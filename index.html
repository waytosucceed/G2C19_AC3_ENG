Subject and Predicate

Every sentence has two main parts: a subject and a predicate.

The subject is the 'who' or 'what' of the sentence. It's the star!
Examples:

  • The cat
  • My friend
  • The big tree

The predicate is what the subject is doing or what's happening to it. It's the action!
Examples:

  • sleeps on the bed
  • runs fast
  • has green leaves

When we put them together, we get a full sentence:

  • The cat sleeps on the bed.
  • My friend runs fast.
  • The big tree has green leaves.
